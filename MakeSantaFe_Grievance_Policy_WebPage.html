Purpose & Scope

Make Santa Fe is committed to fostering a safe, inclusive, and equitable environment where every member, guest, instructor, volunteer, and staff person can learn, build, and thrive. This policy establishes a clear, transparent, and trauma-informed process for reporting, investigating, and resolving grievances and conflicts — in alignment with best practices from the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM), the Nonprofit Risk Management Center (NRMC), and DEI frameworks grounded in equity, accessibility, and intersectionality.

This policy applies to all individuals who interact with Make Santa Fe in any capacity: members, staff, instructors, volunteers, vendors, guests, and board members — whether incidents occur on-site, at Make-sponsored events, or in Make-affiliated online spaces.

Living Document This policy will be reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect changes in law, community needs, and organizational learning. It supplements but does not replace applicable New Mexico law, the Make Santa Fe Member Agreement, or board bylaws.

Guiding Principles

All grievance and conflict resolution processes at Make Santa Fe are guided by:

🤝
Dignity & Respect
Every person involved will be treated with care, regardless of the nature of the complaint.
⚖️
Equity & Inclusion
Processes will account for power differentials, language access, and intersectional identities.
🔒
Confidentiality
Information will be protected to the maximum extent possible, disclosed only on a need-to-know basis.
🛡️
Non-Retaliation
Good-faith reporters, participants, and witnesses are fully protected from adverse consequences.
⏱️
Timeliness
Prompt acknowledgment and resolution at every stage of the process.
🌱
Restorative Intent
Where appropriate, the goal is restoration of relationships and community, not only punishment.
💙
Trauma-Informed
Investigators approach all parties with awareness of how trauma affects memory, disclosure, and participation.
Sources SHRM Managing Conflict in the Workplace Toolkit; Nonprofit Risk Management Center, Taking the High Road (2024 ed.); UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, Pillar III.

Protected Categories & Covered Behaviors

3.1 — Protected Categories

Make Santa Fe prohibits discrimination, harassment, or differential treatment on the basis of any characteristic protected under federal or New Mexico law, including but not limited to:

  • Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
  • Sex, gender identity, or gender expression
  • Sexual orientation
  • Age (18+ for membership; all minors receive youth protections)
  • Disability (physical, cognitive, or mental health)
  • Religion or belief system (or lack thereof)
  • Pregnancy or parental status
  • Veteran or military status
  • Socioeconomic status or housing situation
  • Immigration status
  • Neurodivergence

3.2 — Reportable Behaviors

  • Abusive language or behavior
  • Discrimination on the basis of any protected category
  • Harassment, including sexual harassment
  • Unwanted physical contact or threats of physical contact
  • Violence or credible threats of violence
  • Stalking, intimidation, or targeted hostile behavior
  • Retaliation against a reporter or witness
  • Misuse of this process (bad-faith reports)
Note This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. Behavior that creates a hostile, unsafe, or unwelcoming environment for any member of our community falls within scope of this policy, even if not explicitly listed above.

Conflict Resolution & Escalation Pathway

Make Santa Fe uses a tiered approach. The appropriate starting point depends on the nature, severity, and urgency of the situation. Reporters always have the right to bypass lower tiers when the situation warrants it.

1
Informal Resolution
Direct Dialogue
For minor interpersonal disagreements or misunderstandings. Both parties engage in respectful dialogue, with optional staff facilitation. Skip this tier entirely if the incident involves harassment, discrimination, physical contact, threats, or any power imbalance.
⏱ Days 1–7
2
Formal Reporting & Staff Review
Investigation by Staff or ED
Submit a formal report. Make Santa Fe will acknowledge within 5 business days, assign a reviewer, conduct an investigation (interviews, documentation), and issue a written determination. Outcomes include warnings, required training, mediation, or temporary suspension.
⏱ Days 1–14
3
Board-Level Review
Board Review Panel
Automatically triggered for: harassment, discrimination, physical contact, violence/threats; reports against the ED or a board member; appeals of Tier 2 decisions; or when Tier 2 exceeds 14 days. A 3-person Board panel issues a written decision. Outcomes may include permanent membership revocation.
⏱ Days 15–30
4
External Resolution
Third-Party Mediation or Legal Referral
If unresolved after Tier 3, or if the situation involves potential criminal conduct or civil rights violations, Make Santa Fe will provide referrals to the EEOC, NM Human Rights Bureau, or local law enforcement, and will cooperate fully with any external investigation.
⏱ As needed
Safety Override In cases involving credible, immediate safety threats, Make Santa Fe reserves the right to take protective action — including removal from the space — at any tier, at any time. If someone is in immediate danger, call 911 first.

Investigation Standards

All investigations will be conducted in accordance with SHRM best practices for workplace investigations:

  • Impartiality — Investigators must disclose conflicts of interest and recuse if necessary.
  • Thoroughness — All relevant facts, witnesses, and documentation will be considered.
  • Proportionality — The depth of investigation matches the severity of the allegation.
  • Both-party notice — Both reporter and respondent are informed that an investigation is underway and given an opportunity to present their account.
  • Documentation — All steps, interviews, and findings are documented in writing.
  • Confidentiality — Investigation records are restricted to those with a need to know and stored securely.
  • Interim measures — Temporary protective measures may be implemented before an investigation concludes when there is risk of ongoing harm.
Scope Note Make Santa Fe is not a legal authority and cannot compel testimony or impose legal penalties. Internal investigations do not substitute for law enforcement or regulatory action.
6

Confidentiality & Anonymous Reporting

Make Santa Fe will protect the confidentiality of all parties to the maximum extent practicable. Complete confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed where:

  • Disclosure is required by law (e.g., mandatory reporting of child abuse or abuse of a vulnerable adult).
  • The safety of an individual or the community requires disclosure.
  • A fair investigation requires the respondent to know the nature of the allegations against them.

Anonymous reports are accepted and will be investigated to the extent possible. Reporters should understand that anonymous submissions may limit Make Santa Fe's ability to follow up, contact the reporter for clarification, or reach a definitive determination.

7

Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who, in good faith, reports a concern, participates in an investigation, or supports another person through this process is strictly prohibited. Retaliation includes — but is not limited to — exclusion, intimidation, disparagement, adverse changes in access or status, or any conduct that would discourage a reasonable person from reporting.

Any person who believes they have experienced retaliation may report it using the same channels as any other grievance. Substantiated retaliation will be treated as a serious policy violation subject to the full range of disciplinary outcomes, up to and including permanent revocation of membership.

8

Possible Outcomes & Disciplinary Measures

Depending on the nature and severity of a substantiated violation, outcomes may include:

  • Informal counseling or coaching
  • Written warning placed in personnel or membership record
  • Required completion of training (e.g., harassment prevention, bystander intervention)
  • Formal mediation or restorative dialogue (with consent of all parties)
  • Temporary suspension of space access or membership privileges
  • Permanent revocation of membership or access (Board decision)
  • Referral to external authorities for potential legal action
Inconclusive Findings In cases where a determination is inconclusive, Make Santa Fe may still take proactive steps — such as additional community training, structural safeguards, or environmental changes — to prevent future incidents and improve community culture.

Accessibility & Language Access

Make Santa Fe is committed to making this process accessible to everyone. Accommodations and language access support are available upon request, including:

  • Submitting reports in Spanish or requesting a Spanish-speaking point of contact
  • Requesting a support person or advocate to accompany you through the process
  • Receiving written communications in accessible formats
  • Alternative submission methods for individuals with disabilities

Contact build@makesantafe.org to request any accommodation. No accommodation request will be used as evidence in any investigation.
